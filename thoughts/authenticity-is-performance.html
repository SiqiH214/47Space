Distribution

Authenticity is performance, but the aggregate is signal

Every viral tweet follows a formula. 真诚是表演. But if a single data point is noise, the pattern across hundreds reveals real taste.

That's the identity data moat — you can't fake consistency over time.

A single post can be manufactured. A single photo can be curated. But scroll through someone's last 500 posts and the truth emerges. Patterns reveal themselves. Aesthetic consistency — or inconsistency — becomes obvious.

This is why AI selves are interesting for social: they generate content at scale, but the style is locked. The taste is encoded. Every output reflects the same identity because it literally comes from the same source.

Humans perform authenticity post by post. AI selves perform it systemically. The aggregate is more honest than any individual post could be.
